Why PLFS was Revamped in 2025

Primary Objective: Enable higher-frequency labour force indicators with improved accuracy and granularity
  • Higher Frequency Indicators: Monthly all-India CWS (Current Weekly Status), quarterly rural+urban CWS, and annual usual status estimates
  • Rotational Panel Design: 4 visits per household over 4 consecutive months for better trend capture
  • Enhanced Sample Size: 22,692 FSUs per year (12,504 rural + 10,188 urban)
  • Increased Household Coverage: 12 households per FSU (up from 8 in previous design)
  • Improved Representation: Better coverage of educated households through Second Stage Stratification (SSS)
  • Post-Census Continuity: Two-year cycle with frame updates ensuring data consistency
Source: MoSPI Changes in PLFS Document (2025) | Effective: January 2025 onwards

Overview of Rotational Panel Design

Visit Structure

  • First Visit: Comprehensive baseline data collection
  • Revisits 1-3: Change tracking + current status (Months 2-4)
  • Total Duration: 4 consecutive months per household
  • Panel Rotation: Fresh panels introduced monthly

Overlap Benefits

  • 75% Month-to-Month Overlap: Ensures continuity in trend analysis
  • 50% Quarter-to-Quarter Overlap: Stable quarterly estimates
  • Fresh Listing: Only in first month of new panel
  • Frame Updates: Continuous alignment with census frames
Key Advantage: This design enables production of monthly unemployment rates while maintaining data quality through panel stability, balancing timeliness with statistical reliability.

Schedule 0.0PL – Purpose & When Used

Primary Function

Listing schedule for households in selected First Stage Units (FSUs) to enable scientific selection of sample households for Schedule 10.4

When to Use Schedule 0.0PL

  • New Panel Introduction: Used ONLY when a new panel is introduced (first month of rotation)
  • Fresh Listing Required: Complete enumeration of all households in the selected FSU
  • Stratification Basis: Enables formation of Second Stage Strata (SSS) based on education levels
  • Sample Selection: Facilitates selection of 12 sample households per FSU for detailed survey
Not Used in Revisits: During revisits (months 2-4), use existing listing or prepare fresh only if major structural changes occur in the FSU (e.g., natural disasters, large-scale migration)

Schedule 0.0PL – Key Structural Elements (1/2)

Block 0: Descriptive Identification

  • State and District names/codes
  • Village/Town identification
  • Tehsil/Taluk/Block details
  • Landmark information
  • FSU location specifics

Block 1: Identification Particulars

  • Sample Number (unique identifier)
  • Sector Code: 1=Rural, 2=Urban
  • NSS Region code
  • Stratum and Sub-stratum numbers
  • Sub-Unit (SU) and Sub-division details
  • Second Stage Stratum (SSS) code
  • Frame Code and Survey Code
  • Month/Year and Panel identifiers
Important Codes: Inhabited=1 | Casualty/Substitute cases documented with reason codes | Ensure all codes match master frame

Schedule 0.0PL – Key Structural Elements (2/2)

Geographic Formation Blocks

Formation Type Criteria Action Required
Hamlet/SU Formation (Rural) Large villages with distinct pockets List all hamlets by population %, form SUs, randomly select one SU for survey
Sub-division Formation Population ≥1,500 (or ≥750 special cases) List all sub-divisions, select one randomly using prescribed procedure

Block 5: Household Listing

  • Serial Number: Continuous numbering of all households
  • House Number: Physical address/location identifier
  • Head of Household: Name of primary member
  • Household Size: Total members usually residing
  • Secondary+ Educated Members: Count of members with education ≥ 10th standard (critical for SSS formation)
Probe Thoroughly: Education information determines stratification. Verify certificates or ask multiple sources if uncertain.

Schedule 0.0PL – Second Stage Strata (SSS)

Basis: Members with general education ≥ Secondary level (10th standard pass and above)

Rural Areas (3 SSS)

SSS Criteria HH Selected
SSS 1 2+ educated members 4 hh
SSS 2 Exactly 1 educated 6 hh
SSS 3 0 educated 2 hh

Urban Areas (4 SSS)

SSS Criteria HH Selected
SSS 1 3+ educated 4 hh
SSS 2 Exactly 2 educated 3 hh
SSS 3 Exactly 1 educated 3 hh
SSS 4 0 educated 2 hh
Total: 12 households selected per FSU | Objective: Ensure adequate representation of educated households in labour force estimates

Schedule 0.0PL – Household Selection Process

Step-by-Step Selection Procedure

1
Complete Listing: Enumerate ALL households in selected SU/FSU without omission
2
Education Count: For each household, count members with secondary education (≥10th standard)
3
SSS Formation: Group households into respective SSS categories based on education count
4
Cumulative Totals: Prepare cumulative list of households within each SSS
5
Random Selection: Apply systematic random sampling using prescribed random numbers
6
Final Sample: Select specified number of households from each SSS (total 12 hh)
Quality Check: Verify that selected households are geographically distributed across the FSU to avoid clustering bias

Schedule 0.0PL – Special Guidelines & Cases

Handling Special Situations

Situation Guideline SSS Assignment
Locked Households Probe neighbors for information; make multiple visits at different times Default to SSS 3 (Rural) or SSS 4 (Urban) if education info unavailable
Paying Guests Often treated as separate single-member households if independently cooking Based on individual's education level
Substitutes Only during initial selection if original household refuses or is casualty Same SSS as original
Approximate Population For SU/hamlet formation, consult knowledgeable persons (sarpanch, teachers, anganwadi workers) N/A
Documentation: Record reasons for substitutions, locked households, and any deviations from standard procedure in remarks column
Critical: Never assume education levels based on appearance or occupation. Always verify through direct inquiry or documentation.
